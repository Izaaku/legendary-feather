Frequently asked questions

How does Legendary Feather work for phone calls?
For travelers, the app translates between two people standing face-to-face — you speak, the other person hears the translation through your phone's speaker. For business / contact centers, our Virtual Audio Driver (Windows / Mac) creates a virtual microphone that any softphone — Zoom, Teams, Twilio Flex, Five9, RingCentral — can use, so the customer hears your voice in their language in real time.
Can I use the app to translate a regular phone call?
PWAs and mobile apps cannot intercept native phone calls — that's a hard limitation imposed by iOS and Android. For real call translation you need either our Virtual Audio Driver (for desktop softphones) or our upcoming Twilio bridge (for inbound numbers).
What's the difference between standard and premium voice minutes?
Standard voice is fast, low-latency, sounds natural in 100+ languages. Premium voice is hyper-realistic, studio-quality, and supports voice cloning so the translation sounds like YOUR voice. Premium minutes are limited per plan because they cost ~5× more — most plans give you a generous quota for the moments that matter (sales demos, important customer calls).
Do you offer custom contracts for large BPOs?
Yes — the Enterprise tier covers everything: dedicated deployment in your cloud or an isolated Railway instance, SOC 2 / HIPAA / PCI compliance, custom voice models trained on your audio, integration with your PBX (Twilio Flex, Five9, Genesys), 24/7 phone support, and SLA penalties in contract. Click "Talk to Sales" above and we'll set up a call within 1 business day.
Can I cancel anytime?
Yes. Monthly subscriptions cancel at the end of the current billing period — no penalties. The Travel Pass is a one-time purchase that simply expires after 7 days. Enterprise contracts have annual terms with discounted pricing.
What if I exceed my monthly minutes?
You can either upgrade your plan or buy add-on minute packs at your plan's overage rate. We never auto-charge or suspend your account without warning — you'll get a notification at 80% usage.
