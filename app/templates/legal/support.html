Polyglot Desk — Browser Extension

How do I set up my API keys?

Polyglot Desk uses a Bring-Your-Own-Keys (BYOK) model. After installing the extension:

  1. Click the Polyglot Desk icon in your Chrome toolbar to open the popup.
  2. Click API Keys at the top.
  3. Paste your keys for the providers you want to use:
    • Ollama Cloud (required for grammar, rewriting, translation) — get a key here
    • Deepgram (required for voice dictation) — get a key here — $200 free credit on signup
    • Cartesia (recommended for TTS) — get a key here — fastest voice synthesis
    • ElevenLabs (optional TTS fallback) — get a key here
    • Mapbox (optional — better address geocoding) — get a key here — 100K reqs/mo free
  4. Click Save after each key. Your keys are stored only in your browser's local storage and never sent to our servers.

How do I use voice dictation?

Press Alt + Shift + M (Windows/Linux) or Ctrl + Shift + M (Mac) on any web page with a text field. A dictation popup appears and begins recording immediately. Press the same shortcut again or click Stop to end. Your transcript appears in the popup and can be inserted into the focused field with the Insert button.

How does Universal Translator work?

Inside the dictation popup, click 🌐 Universal Translator, choose your target language (Spanish, English, French, German, Italian, Portuguese, Russian, Japanese, Chinese, Korean, Hindi, Arabic, and 18 more), and start speaking. Your speech is transcribed, translated, and read aloud in the target language — useful for live multilingual conversations and customer service calls.

Why isn't grammar correction working?

Most often this is because the Ollama key is missing or invalid. Open the extension popup, check API Keys, and confirm your Ollama key shows a green ✓. If it's red, paste a fresh key from ollama.com/settings/keys and save again. If the issue persists, open DevTools (F12) on any page and look for any red [Polyglot ...] errors — share them with us.

The microphone isn't picking up my voice

Chrome requires explicit microphone permission per site. When the dictation popup says "Mic access denied":

  1. Click the 🔒 padlock in Chrome's URL bar.
  2. Click Site settings.
  3. Change Microphone from "Ask" or "Block" to "Allow" (not "Allow this time" — that expires when you reload).
  4. Close and reopen the tab.

Why doesn't it work in Google Docs?

Google Docs uses a <canvas>-based editor rather than the standard HTML text fields, which means live grammar underlines and hover-to-translate can't paint inside the document. The extension shows a one-time banner in Docs explaining this. The features that DO work in Docs: right-click → Polyglot Desk: Correct Selection, and voice dictation via Alt+Shift+M (the transcript goes to your clipboard so you can paste it).

Are my conversations stored on your servers?

No. In the Free (BYOK) tier, your audio, text, and translations are sent directly from your browser to the AI providers you configured (Ollama, Deepgram, Cartesia, ElevenLabs). They don't pass through Legendary Feather's servers at all. We have zero telemetry, zero analytics, zero trackers in the Free tier. See our Polyglot Desk Privacy Policy for the full details.
